Common Flight Control System (C-FCS)


We are developing C-FCS for safe control of aircraft ranging from small hand-launched unmanned aircraft to large, manned aircraft by leveraging the capability of plug-and-play sensors, processors, and actuators. A common system allows for standardization across a fleet of varying types of aircraft which reduces training, maintenance, logistics and upgrade costs. C-FCS can also facilitate interoperability between various aircraft, assist with research efforts, and help to streamline the airworthiness certification process.

Functionality


C-FCS works by flexibly integrating sensors, actuators, and processors on a common network, enabling configuring the system for a wide variety of different vehicles. Redundant sensors, actuators, and processors can be added to provide redundancy for safety-critical and manned vehicles. Fault tolerant control software maximally utilizes the system redundancy, autonomously detecting faults and modifying control laws, to enable the vehicle to safely continue flight. A cascaded flight control system supports multiple modes of operation: fully-autonomous, remotely-piloted, and piloted. Handling qualities are evaluated to ensure safe vehicle operation, even in degraded vehicle states




Air Force Partnership


We were awarded an AFWERX, Agility Prime Phase I Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) fall 2020 for further improvement and customer exploration. Our partners include Pennsylvania State University (PSU), Systems Technology Inc (STI) and Beta Technologies. PSU bring expertise in the areas of autonomous fault-detection and reconfiguration, STI brings expertise in control law design and handling qualities analysis, and Beta Technologies brings unique feedback as a potential customer and developer of a manned eVTOL vehicle. Together we are creating a fault-tolerant, plug-and-play common flight control system.
