Common Flight Control System (C-FCS)

  • Fully-autonomous, remotely-piloted, and piloted modes of operation
    • Cascaded control laws
  • Enhanced safety through redundancy
    • Rapid scaling ability
    • Plug-and-play sensors, processors, and actuators
    • Intelligent identification of redundant components
  • Fault-tolerant control software
    • autonomous fault detection and control law modification for safe flight
  • Common set of hardware, software, and development tools across a varying fleet of aircraft
    • Quick commercialization of new technologies
    • Ease of maintenance and training
    • End-user standardization
    • Streamlined airworthiness certification process
    • Interoperability between vehicles
  • Research and flight test capabilities

Air Force Partnership

AFRL's Power and Controls Division (AFRL/RQQC) of the Aerospace Systems Directorate has partnered with us for C-FCS development and is very interested in the technology for their program development and AFSIM integration. C-FCS will provide a system with well-understood performance characteristics, risk levels, and methods of integration for their Air Force customers. C-FCS will also provide them flexible architecture and open-source software for quick-turnaround projects with the Air Force Program Executive Offices (PEO) that request their expertise. Projects can be tested on their in-house aircraft and a standard control system will allow for quick delivery of solutions to meet the warfighter needs.
